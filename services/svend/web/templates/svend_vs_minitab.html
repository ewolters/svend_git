Bayesian SPC — Beyond the 3-Sigma Rule

Traditional SPC uses fixed 3-sigma control limits based on assumptions that often don't hold. Svend's Bayesian Process Belief System replaces these with posterior-driven monitoring that adapts to your data:

  • Bayesian Cpk — Instead of a single number, get a full posterior distribution. Ask "What is the probability that Cpk exceeds 1.33?" and get a direct answer. No 1.5σ shift assumption needed. Try the free calculator.
  • Adaptive control limits — Limits that shrink as data accumulates and your estimate of the process improves. More data means tighter bounds — not the same fixed lines forever.
  • Online changepoint detection — Bayesian Online Changepoint Detection (BOCPD, Adams & MacKay 2007) identifies process shifts as they happen, observation by observation, without batch recomputation.
  • Anytime-valid sequential testing — E-values (Grünwald et al. 2024) let you continuously monitor a test and stop whenever the evidence is strong enough. No penalty for "peeking" at the data.
  • Bayesian DOE — Screen effects by P(practical significance) rather than p < 0.05. Compare models via exact marginal likelihood instead of AIC/BIC approximations. Run sequential experiments guided by information gain.
  • Bayesian Gage R&R — Gibbs sampler produces posterior distributions for every variance component. Get P(%GRR < 10%) and credible intervals on NDC, not just point estimates. Sequential updating lets each new study inherit the prior from the last one.
  • Decision-theoretic alarms — Set alarm thresholds based on the actual cost of missed shifts vs false alarms, not arbitrary significance levels.

This is the direction quality engineering is heading. Svend is the only platform implementing it today.

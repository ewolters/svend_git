## **1. SCOPE AND PURPOSE**

### **1.1 Purpose**

RDM-001 defines the structure, lifecycle, and transparency requirements for Svend's public product roadmap. Every planned feature is tracked as a `RoadmapItem` with a defined area, quarter, status, and minimum pricing tier. The roadmap serves dual purposes: internal planning discipline and external trust signal.

**Core Principle:**

> A roadmap is a public contract. What you promise, you ship or you explain.
> Every item has a quarter, a status, and a trail. Stale promises are flagged automatically.

### **1.2 Scope**

**Applies to:**
- `RoadmapItem` model in `api/models.py`
- Internal dashboard roadmap tab (staff CRUD)
- Public `/roadmap/` page (customer-facing)
- `check_roadmap` compliance check in `syn/audit/compliance.py`

**Does NOT apply to:**
- Internal-only technical debt items (tracked in `.kjerne/DEBT.md`)
- Sprint-level task tracking (out of scope)
- Marketing announcements not backed by roadmap items

## **3. TERMINOLOGY**

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Roadmap Item** | A planned feature or initiative tracked as a `RoadmapItem` record with area, quarter, status, and tier. |
| **Epic** | A large body of work decomposable into multiple roadmap items sharing an area and quarter. |
| **Quarterly Cycle** | The planning cadence: each calendar quarter (Q1-Q4) defines a planning horizon for roadmap commitments. |
| **Area** | A product surface taxonomy code (e.g., `dsw`, `qms`, `platform`) classifying roadmap items. |
| **Tier** | The minimum pricing tier (free, professional, team, enterprise) required to access a shipped feature. |
| **Scope Lock** | The compliance rule that past-quarter `planned` items must be resolved — they cannot remain indefinitely unaddressed. |

## **5. STATUS LIFECYCLE**

<!-- assert: Status choices include planned, in_progress, shipped, deferred, cancelled | check=rdm-statuses -->
<!-- impl: api/models.py:RoadmapItem -->
<!-- test: syn.audit.tests.test_roadmap.RoadmapModelTest.test_status_choices -->

| Status | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| `planned` | Committed to a quarter, not yet started |
| `in_progress` | Active development underway |
| `shipped` | Delivered to production |
| `deferred` | Postponed to a future quarter |
| `cancelled` | Removed from roadmap permanently |

<!-- assert: Status transitions follow defined lifecycle — no backward transitions from shipped | check=rdm-valid-transitions -->
<!-- impl: api/internal_views.py -->
<!-- test: syn.audit.tests.test_roadmap.RoadmapModelTest.test_status_choices -->
<!-- test: syn.audit.tests.test_roadmap.ShippedTrackingTest.test_shipped_requires_shipped_at -->

**Valid Transitions:**

```
planned → in_progress → shipped
planned → deferred
planned → cancelled
in_progress → shipped
in_progress → deferred
```

Items cannot transition backward (e.g., `shipped` cannot return to `in_progress`). Deferred items may be re-planned in a future quarter by creating a new roadmap item.

### **5.1 Shipped Tracking**

<!-- assert: Shipped items must have shipped_at timestamp | check=rdm-shipped-tracking -->
<!-- impl: api/internal_views.py -->
<!-- test: syn.audit.tests.test_roadmap.ShippedTrackingTest.test_shipped_requires_shipped_at -->

When an item transitions to `shipped`, the `shipped_at` timestamp is set automatically to the current time. The compliance check flags any shipped item missing this timestamp as a warning.

### **5.2 Scope Locking**

<!-- assert: Past-quarter planned items flagged by compliance check | check=rdm-scope-lock -->
<!-- impl: syn/audit/compliance.py -->
<!-- test: syn.audit.tests.test_roadmap.ComplianceCheckTest.test_stale_items_flagged -->

Items with a `quarter` in the past that remain in `planned` status indicate scope drift. The `check_roadmap` compliance check flags these as failures requiring resolution — the item must be transitioned to `deferred`, `cancelled`, or `shipped`.

## **6. QUARTERLY PLANNING CYCLE**

### **6.1 Upcoming Quarter**

<!-- assert: Upcoming quarter must have at least one roadmap item | check=rdm-upcoming -->
<!-- impl: syn/audit/compliance.py -->
<!-- test: syn.audit.tests.test_roadmap.ComplianceCheckTest.test_empty_upcoming_warning -->

The next calendar quarter must have at least one roadmap item. An empty upcoming quarter triggers a compliance warning, indicating that planning has not been completed.

### **6.2 Review Cadence**

At quarter boundaries:
1. Review all `planned` items for the ending quarter — transition to `shipped`, `deferred`, or `cancelled`
2. Confirm at least one item exists for the upcoming quarter
3. Run `check_roadmap` to verify no stale items remain

<!-- impl: syn/audit/compliance.py -->
<!-- test: syn.audit.tests.test_roadmap.ComplianceCheckTest.test_check_registered -->

`check_roadmap` is registered in `ALL_CHECKS` and runs as part of the daily compliance suite. It verifies:

| Check | Severity | Condition |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Stale items | **Fail** | Past-quarter items still in `planned` status |
| Shipped tracking | **Warning** | Shipped items missing `shipped_at` timestamp |
| Empty upcoming quarter | **Warning** | No items exist for the next calendar quarter |

**SOC 2 Mapping:** CC9.1 (Change Management) — the roadmap provides forward-looking visibility into planned changes, and the compliance check ensures commitments are tracked to resolution.

## **9. ANTI-PATTERNS**

### **9.1 Roadmap Items Without Status**

**PROHIBITED:** Creating roadmap items that lack a defined status or quarter assignment.

Every `RoadmapItem` MUST have a valid `status` from the lifecycle (§5) and a `quarter` in `Q[1-4]-YYYY` format. Items without these fields provide no planning signal and cannot be tracked by the compliance check.

### **9.2 Backdated Planning**

**PROHIBITED:** Creating roadmap items with quarters in the past to fabricate a planning history.

Roadmap items MUST be created for the current or future quarters. Past-quarter items in `planned` status are flagged as stale by `check_roadmap` — they indicate scope drift, not retroactive planning.

### **9.3 Shadow Roadmaps**

**PROHIBITED:** Maintaining feature plans outside the `RoadmapItem` system (spreadsheets, ad-hoc documents, verbal commitments).

All planned features visible to customers or used in internal planning MUST exist as `RoadmapItem` records. The compliance check, public transparency page, and audit trail only cover items in the system.

What PBS sees that classical SPC cannot

1
Pooling hides structure

Classical SPC computes one Cpk from all 180 observations, mixing four material lots with different behavior. The grand mean looks centered; the pooled σ looks small. PBS segments by known covariates (lot, operator, machine) and runs Bayesian Cpk per segment. Two lots fail individually even though the pool passes.

2
Point estimates lie about uncertainty

Classical Cpk = 1.65 is a single number with no confidence interval. PBS gives P(Cpk > 1.33) = 4% for LOT-001 — a posterior probability that honestly quantifies how sure we are. The point estimate looked safe. The full posterior says it isn't.

3
Shifts inside control limits are invisible

The process shift at observation 54 never triggered a Western Electric rule because it wasn't large enough to breach 3σ limits. BOCPD (Bayesian Online Changepoint Detection) detected it at P = 52% by observation 59 and confirmed it by observation 65. The shift was real — it just wasn't dramatic.

4
Loss isn't binary

Classical SPC says in-spec or out-of-spec. Taguchi Loss says every deviation from target costs money, and decomposes that cost into bias (off-center) vs variance (spread). Regime 2 costs $0.184/unit — 6.5× more than Regime 1 — because the post-shift bias dominates. Different regimes need different corrective actions: center it vs reduce spread.
